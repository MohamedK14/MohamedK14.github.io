Welcome — Computer Science Student

Mohamed Kourouma.

Ann Arbor, MI  ·  B.Sc. Computer Science  ·  University of Detroit Mercy  ·  Expected May 2028

I'm a Computer Science student originally from Guinea, West Africa, driven by one mission: to build software that makes people's lives genuinely better. I write code that works, ask questions that matter, and treat every project as an opportunity to grow.

My path into CS has been anything but ordinary. I initially started as self-taught — using FreeCodeCamp, Codecademy, and DataCamp to build a foundation before my formal coursework, and I continue to push beyond what the classroom alone can offer. That drive has led me to build projects ranging from AI-powered language apps and real-time aircraft tracking systems to game-playing AI using Minimax and A* algorithms.

Alongside my studies, I worked as a STEM tutor at Washtenaw Community College, helping students break down complex concepts — an experience that sharpened my ability to communicate technical ideas clearly and reinforced the fundamentals I rely on every day as a developer.

I am currently seeking a co-op or internship in software engineering where I can contribute, collaborate, and keep learning from people who care about building things well. Use the navigation above to explore my work, or jump straight in below.
